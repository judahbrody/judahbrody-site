Rip Current Sports

From local e-store to Amazon partner. Video that sold the products and sharpened the brand.

Client and context

Rip Current Sports is a sports brand that sells its gear direct to customers online. They came in as a growing e-store looking to lift sales and raise how their products were presented.

The problem

A small online store competing on presentation. Long recordings sat unused, product pages were missing the kind of video that builds buyer confidence, and the brand needed a steady, affordable stream of content to keep momentum across social.

What we did

Two tiers of editing

Fast, low-cost social edits kept the feed active, while heavier paid-playlist edits trimmed and polished long recordings into tight, watchable pieces. Matching the effort and the price to each job kept the output sustainable for a growing brand.

Instructional video that sells

Built instructional videos that double as product marketing. Showing the gear in real use supports new launches and gives buyers the confidence to click purchase. See it on the Rip Current lessons and trainings page.

Promos and graphics

Cut event promos and produced light graphics to round out a consistent brand presence.

Craft

Most of the work is in the edit. A strong hook up front, tight trimming so people keep watching, clean sound, and consistent color so every piece looks like the same brand made it.

The result

The brand grew from a local e-store into an Amazon partner, with stronger product presentation through instructional video and a steady content pipeline supporting sales and new product launches.

“Our expectations were exceeded from day one of working with Judah. He helped grow us from a small e-store into an Amazon partner.”

Jose Gallagher, CEO, Rip Current Sports
